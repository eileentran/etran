うま味

/uːˈmaːmi/

from Japan in 1908

"savory"

×

People taste umami through taste receptors that respond to glutamatic acids. Glutamic acids occur naturally in foods or can be added like a seasoning.

In the body, glutamic acid is often found as glutamate, a neurotransmitter. Glutamate is one of the most abundant neurotransmitters in the brain, playing a crucial role in brain development.
